What to Bring to the California Real Estate Exam

Two things: your printed Exam Schedule Notice and one government-issued photo ID. The list of what you can't bring is a lot longer. Here's the whole exam-day walkthrough, DRE rules included.

The Short Answer

The Packing List Is Two Items

🎒
Short Answer
Bring your Exam Schedule Notice — printed from eLicensing, barcode and all — and one valid photo ID: a current state-issued driver's license or DMV ID card, a passport, or a U.S. military ID. That's the whole list. You will not be admitted without the ID, and DRE accepts exactly those forms.

California runs its own exam — no Pearson VUE, no third-party test centers. You'll sit at a DRE electronic exam center in one of five cities: Fresno, La Palma, Oakland, Sacramento, or San Diego. (And no, it's not offered online — DRE says exam security is the reason.) Because the state runs the show, the rules come straight from DRE's examinee instructions, and they're stricter than most people expect. Here's what to have in hand:

1
Your Exam Schedule Notice
Print it

When you schedule through DRE's eLicensing system, you print yourself a barcoded schedule notice — that's your ticket. Print it the day you book, not the morning of, and know your exact session time: sessions begin promptly as scheduled.

2
One Photo ID — From a Short List
Required

DRE accepts three forms: a current driver's license or DMV identification card, a passport (U.S. or foreign-government issued), or a U.S. military ID. Note the word current — expired doesn't count, and nothing else substitutes. No ID, no admission, full stop.

⚠️

Life happens — reschedule ahead, don't no-show cold. Exam fees are never refunded (that's statute, not policy), but a missed or moved date isn't a disaster: you rebook through eLicensing for the $40 reschedule fee. And if you're suddenly free, eLicensing lets qualified examinees grab an open seat as late as 6:00 a.m. the day of the exam.

What Not to Bring

Everything Else Goes in the Locker

DRE's prohibited-items list reads like a TSA checkpoint: cell phones, purses, wallets, keys, backpacks, food, drink, gum, study materials, tablets, laptops, cameras, watches, your own pens, lapel pins, even hats. Phones get powered off and placed in a locker (or a storage table when lockers run out), everything else is stored away from you, and DRE takes no responsibility for lost or stolen items — so the less you walk in with, the better.

Two rules worth knowing before they surprise you: merely possessing a cell phone during the exam is prohibited — including while out on a break — and you can't leave the exam room without a proctor's express permission. Wondering about the one gadget everyone asks about? That has its own guide.

The Walkthrough

How Exam Day Actually Goes

1
Arrive 30 Minutes Early
T-minus 30

DRE's own instruction: arrive 30 minutes before your session. Check-in means verifying your ID against your file — if you wear a mask, you'll briefly remove it so staff can confirm it's you — and getting your belongings stored. Then you're seated at a testing station.

2
The Exam Itself
3 hours

150 multiple-choice questions, four answer choices each, 3 hours, 70% to pass. It's all on screen at an electronic testing station. Pace math: 72 seconds per question buys you the full three hours — most people finish with time to spare, so use it to flag and revisit. Full format breakdown: the exam facts guide.

3
Your Result, On Screen
Same day

Electronic exams show your result when you finish. One DRE quirk: pass, and they won't tell you your score — you just pass. Fail, and you get the number plus a breakdown. (The DRE has its ways.) A fail also isn't fatal: your application stays valid for two years and the retake process is a $100 fee and a new date.

💡

The two-item packing list is the easy part. Nothing you bring — or forget — moves your score. The 150 questions are the whole game, and they're the one thing on this page you can actually prepare for.

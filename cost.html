Short Answer
Plan on roughly $600–$900 total. The state's cut is fixed — $100 exam fee and $350 license fee. The variable is your 135 hours of required pre-license courses, which run anywhere from about $100 to $400 online for the same state-approved content. Add ~$70–80 for fingerprinting and you have the whole picture.

DRE fees per the official DRE fee schedule, current as of July 2026. You can pay the exam and license fees together as a combined application: $450.

The course price range is real, and the spread has almost nothing to do with quality of outcome. Every approved provider teaches to the same DRE-mandated curriculum — Real Estate Principles, Real Estate Practice, and one elective, 45 hours each. Expensive packages mostly add production polish and upsells, not different material.

The Oops Budget

Fees You Only Pay If Things Go Sideways

ItemCost
Re-taking the exam (per attempt)$100

Worth knowing: DRE fees are nonrefundable. A no-show or a failed attempt doesn't come back as a credit. With the statewide pass rate hovering around half, the single highest-leverage money move in this whole table is not paying the $100 retake fee — which is a preparation question, not a fee question.

💡

The math on preparation: a failed attempt costs $100 plus weeks of waiting to reschedule. Serious prep costs less than that and you only have to do it once. Cheap insurance.

After the License

Costs That Show Up Once You're Licensed

Not part of getting the license, but budget-relevant if you plan to practice: most new agents join a brokerage (splits vary, upfront cost usually $0), and if you'll be doing MLS-based sales you'll encounter local association dues and MLS fees — typically a few hundred to over a thousand per year depending on your board. Those are working-agent costs, not exam costs, and they can wait until you've passed.

The Bottom Line

Cheaper Than One College Class — If You Pass Once

All-in, a California real estate license costs less than a single unit at most universities. The fixed costs are fixed; the only numbers you control are the course price and the retake line. Choose an affordable course, prepare like you mean it, and the whole thing stays a three-digit purchase.

Short Answer
150 questions, 3 hours, 70% to pass. The California salesperson exam is 150 multiple-choice questions in a single 3-hour sitting. You need 105 correct to pass. Every question is worth the same, there's no essay section, and there's no penalty for guessing — so you answer all 150, always.
The Format

What You're Actually Walking Into

The exam is computer-based and administered by the DRE. Every question is multiple choice with four options and exactly one defensibly correct answer. There's no math-by-hand section, no essays, and no calculator — the numbers stay simple on purpose.

Do the time math and the pressure eases a little: 3 hours across 150 questions is 72 seconds per question. Most questions take far less, which banks time for the long scenario ones. Running out of clock is rarely what sinks people — not knowing the vocabulary is.

Scoring

The 70% Pass Line and a Scoring Quirk

You need 105 of 150. Here's the quirk: if you pass, the DRE doesn't tell you your score. A pass is a pass — there's no transcript bragging about your 92%. If you fail, you get a score breakdown so you know where you fell short (which, silver lining, is exactly the study plan for round two).

🎯

The statewide pass rate hovers right around half. So, statistically, a coin flip — except unlike a coin flip, you can rig this one in advance. Prepared first-time candidates pass at meaningfully higher rates than the average, which includes a lot of people who walked in cold.
